Final Recommendations
1. UX & Website Optimization

The checkout experience is performing extremely well. The conversion rate from Checkout Start → Purchase is above ~80%, which indicates the payment flow is smooth and users are not encountering major friction at this stage.

Recommendation

Avoid redesigning or modifying the checkout flow for now.

Any major UX changes here could introduce unnecessary risk and negatively impact conversions.

Key Insight

If a funnel stage is already performing at a very high conversion rate, optimization efforts should be directed toward earlier stages of the customer journey instead.

2. Marketing Strategy

Traffic analysis shows a clear imbalance between traffic volume and conversion efficiency across acquisition channels.

Key Findings

Social Media

Drives ~30% of total traffic

Has the lowest conversion rate

Likely attracting high-volume but low-intent users

Email Marketing

Highest performing acquisition channel

~13% conversion rate compared to ~6% for Social

Recommendations

Reduce budget allocated to traffic-focused social media campaigns

Shift strategy toward:

Retargeting campaigns

Lead generation campaigns

The goal should be capturing users rather than forcing immediate purchases.

Actionable Opportunity

Implement an email capture popup for social media visitors.

Once users are on the email list, they enter a channel with significantly higher purchase probability.

3. Financial & Revenue Optimization

Revenue analysis shows the Average Order Value (AOV) is approximately $115.

This allows us to establish a healthy Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) threshold.

Recommendation

Set a strict CAC limit of $30–$40 per customer.

If social media ads are exceeding this acquisition cost while maintaining low conversion rates, those campaigns are likely unprofitable on a per-customer basis.
